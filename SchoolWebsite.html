INTRODUCTION

In this digital era, no school can claim not to have an online presence. A website forms an information and communication involvement core, easily accessible to students, parents, staff members, and the community at large. Whether you are setting up a new website or refreshing an old one, this tutorial will take you through all that you need to know when it comes to putting up your school website for increased visibility of the school and more functionality for the users.

WHAT IS Website

The school website is an institutional cyber portal regarding academic programs, events, policies, and many more. It should be one avenue in showcasing the achievements the school has undertaken to promote communication and valued resources to prospective and current students, parents, and staff.

A well-designed school website will act as a digital brochure, a communications hub, and even a learning tool. It's key in ensuring that all the stakeholders have been kept abreast of relevant information, allowed to make inquiries, and gleaned an overview with ease.

HOW TO CRAFT A SCHOOL WEBSITE

A number of forethoughts are required in planning an effective and catching school website. Here's how you go about it in steps:

Key Modules to Choose:

  • Set up your goals: Clearly identify what you want to achieve through the website-be this improved communication, increased enrollment, or even offering e-learning resources.
  • Include the Following Pages: In the design and development of webpages, ensure that most of the following, if not all, are included in your website design:
    • Home Page: Snapshot of the mission of the school and other information
    • About Us: Information on the history of the school, values, and achievements
    • Admission: Information on application processes, fees, and timelines.
    • Academic Programs: Information about the Curriculum and Extracurricular Activities.
    • News & Events: What is happening at the school.
    • Contact Us: How to reach us easily with maps and contact forms.
  • Mobile Responsive Layout: The code should assure that the website works perfectly on every device, especially mobile phones.
  • User-Friendly Navigation: Organize the content into categories that make the most sense to the user to allow them to find what they want fast.
  • Security: Include SSL certificates and secure payment gateways to safeguard online transactions of personal information.
